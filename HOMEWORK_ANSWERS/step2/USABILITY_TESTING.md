# Usability Testing Tasks - Mi Sitio

## Instructions for Test Participants

Thank you for participating in this usability test! Your feedback will help us improve the website. Please follow these guidelines:

- **Think out loud** - Share your thoughts and observations as you navigate the site
- **There are no wrong answers** - We're testing the website, not you
- **Take your time** - Work at your own pace
- **Be honest** - Your honest feedback is valuable

---

## Task Set 1: Finding a Product

### Task 1.1: Basic Navigation
**Scenario:** You're visiting the website for the first time and want to see what products are available.

**Your task:** Navigate to the products page and tell us what you see.

**Questions to consider:**
- How easy was it to find the products section?
- What was your first impression of the products page?

---

### Task 1.2: Finding a Specific Product
**Scenario:** You remember seeing "Producto B" mentioned somewhere and want to find it on the website.

**Your task:** Find "Producto B" and tell us what information you can see about it (price, description, etc.).

**Questions to consider:**
- How did you go about finding this product?
- Was the product information clear and easy to understand?

---

### Task 1.3: Finding Products by Price Range
**Scenario:** You have a budget of $30 and want to see which products fit within your budget.

**Your task:** Find all products that cost $30 or less.

**Questions to consider:**
- How easy was it to identify products within your budget?
- What information helped you make this decision?

---

### Task 1.4: Comparing Products
**Scenario:** You're interested in purchasing a product but want to compare your options first.

**Your task:** Look at all available products and tell us which one seems like the best value for money and why.

**Questions to consider:**
- What information did you use to compare the products?
- Was there any information missing that would have helped you decide?

---

### Task 1.5: Understanding Product Details
**Scenario:** You're considering buying "Producto C" and want to learn more about it.

**Your task:** Find "Producto C" and tell us everything you can learn about it from the website.

**Questions to consider:**
- Did you find enough information to make a purchase decision?
- What additional information would you like to see?

---

### Task 1.6: Finding the Cheapest Product
**Scenario:** You're looking for the most affordable option available.

**Your task:** Find the cheapest product on the website.

**Questions to consider:**
- How quickly were you able to identify the cheapest product?
- Was the price information easy to spot?

---

### Task 1.7: Finding Products from the Home Page
**Scenario:** You've just landed on the home page and want to browse products.

**Your task:** Starting from the home page, navigate to view all available products.

**Questions to consider:**
- Was it clear how to get to the products from the home page?
- Did you notice any links or buttons that could help you find products?

---

## Post-Task Questions

After completing all tasks, please answer these general questions:

1. Overall, how easy or difficult was it to find products on this website? (1 = Very Difficult, 5 = Very Easy)
2. What was the most confusing part about finding products?
3. What did you like most about how products are displayed?
4. If you could change one thing about how products are shown, what would it be?
5. Would you feel confident purchasing a product from this website? Why or why not?

---

## Notes for Test Administrators

- Observe where users click and how they navigate
- Note any hesitation or confusion points
- Time how long it takes to complete each task
- Record any verbal feedback or comments
- Pay attention to whether users notice key elements (prices, descriptions, buttons)

## Analysis Framework

### Metrics to Track

**Time Metrics:**
- Average time per task: (Sum of 3 participants ÷ 3)
- Fastest/Slowest time per task

**Success Metrics:**
- Completion rate per task: (Successful completions ÷ 3) × 100
- Overall completion rate: (Total completed tasks ÷ Total tasks) × 100

**Friction Points:**
- Rapid clicking (>3 clicks in <1 second)
- Long pauses (>10 seconds without interaction)
- Back button usage
- Verbal confusion expressions
